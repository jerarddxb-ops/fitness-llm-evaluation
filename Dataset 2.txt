LLM Evaluation Dataset 2
Focus: Fitness domain (increased difficulty, rubric v2 applied)


Task 1
Prompt:
Is eating late at night bad for fat loss?

Response A:
Eating late at night does not inherently cause fat gain. Fat loss is primarily determined by total daily calorie intake, not meal timing.

Response B:
Eating late at night is bad for fat loss because your metabolism slows down, causing food to be stored as fat.

Response C:
Eating late at night can sometimes lead to overeating, which may affect fat loss, but the timing itself is less important than total calorie intake.

Best: A
Ranking: A > C > B
Confidence: 3

Justification:
A is the most succinct and helpful, stating that late night eating is not inherently bad for fat loss and caloric intake is more important. C is similarly accurate, however overeating is not properly explained and could lead to confusion. B makes a misleading claim that if metabolism slows down then food is stored as fat, which is not always true.

Error type:
B: Misleading causal claim and overgeneralization.

Learning signal:
Prefer responses that identify the nuanced relationship between variables rather implying certain variables are more important.
